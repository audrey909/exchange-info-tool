A place to check what was actually said, before repeating it to someone else.

What this is for

Exchange chats fill up fast — housing details, visa steps, form instructions, all mixed in with everyday chatter, and often corrected in a later message that's easy to miss. Checkpoint holds onto that information and answers questions about it directly, so nobody has to scroll back through hundreds of messages or ask a coordinator something that's already been answered.

What's actually happening behind the scenes

Every answer comes from Gemma 4, a small language model running entirely on this computer through Ollama. Nothing is sent anywhere else — no cloud service ever sees your messages. Testing this model surfaced real, specific mistakes it makes (explained below), so the design works around those rather than assuming the model is always right.
